The Proposal

KAT

“It is so hot! Why are you wearing that?” I asked Daniel, as we were getting ready to head out to visit Yoyogi Park. It was another hot and humid sunny day in Tokyo, the kind of day where you would sweat just by the virtue of being outside, and Daniel decides that he'd wear a dress shirt just to go to the park, strange. Anyhow, when we arrived at the Meiji Shrine, it was busy yet serene. Out of nowhere, Daniel spots a private garden and storms over. I scurried in and was immediately empowered by the tranquility of the space. We came up to a tea house overlooking a fishing pond, filled with koi. It was all very poetic. As we got into position for a photo, Daniel turns around and blurts “I love you”, I knew immediately then, that he was proposing. Daniel dropped down on one knee and popped the question! I told him (or at least I think I told him), "yes, and that I cannot not imagine spending the rest of my life with anyone else."

DANIEL

I had originally planned to propose under the giant and most prominent torii outside the Meiji Shrine. It looked perfect in pictures. No people around, intimate, Kat would love it, I thought. When we got there that image quickly vanished as it was about as packed as a Costco on a Sunday afternoon. Equally romantic too. We kept walking and by chance stumbled by the entrance to a beautiful garden. No one was inside because there was an entrance fee. I quickly paid and rushed everyone in. The energy of the place felt right. A small sign described how the former emporer would go there with the empress and fish in the pond. I'm sure there's a metaphor there if you search hard enough. At some point I turned to kat and told her that I love her, that I love her family, that I want to spend the rest of my life with her, and asked her to marry me.
